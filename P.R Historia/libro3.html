Historia de la esclavitud negra en Puerto Rico

In 1873, thirty thousand human beings in Puerto Rico went from slavery to freedom to quickly merge with the free class. Three years later, the black entered into the full enjoyment of his rights, on equal terms with the whites. But why never a general movement of insurrection on the part of the slaves? And how does Puerto Rico manage to emerge as an admirable example of social democracy, where all men contribute with their efforts to the development of the country? History of Black Slavery in Puerto Rico is a first attempt to make known the historical development of the institution of slavery in Puerto Rico, and its repercussions on the immediate reality of Puerto Ricans.
